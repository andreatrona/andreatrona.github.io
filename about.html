Andrea Trona is a sound designer, musician and programmer based in Bologna (IT), graduated in electroacoustic composition at the Cuneo Conservatory and graduated in Sound Design at the Bologna Conservatory. He works with sound in music, performance fields and multimedia works with a strong interest in non-conventional performative spaces, collaborating with artists and performers working as sound director, musician, developer and technician, performing works in Italy and abroad. He has been collaborating since 2019 with Elettronica Collettiva Bologna, the collective of the electronic music school of Bologna, and with the vocal quartet Rebel Bit, receiving awards and participating in European festivals. Since 2020 he has been collaborating permanently with musician and sound designer Francesco Vogli, with whom he developed the TRDmx device as part of a research grant promoted by the Centro Tempo Reale, created self-made light devices and recently founded immòto recordings, a project dedicated to audio recording and abroad. From 2021 he began collaborating on performance and installation projects with sound artist Agnese Banti, such as the performance I versi delle mani by choreographer Marta Bellu with dancer Laura Lucioli, the sound installation LUMINA and a new performance project that will be presented in July 2023 at Santarcangelo Festival. In 2022 he collaborates with Tempo Reale on the project XENAKIS | La Légende d'Eer and presents at Prima Onda Fest 22 the performance BOXE signed with the musician and performer Federico Pipia. In May 2023 his first electroacoustic improvisation album is released by Never Anything Records (US), played with guitarist and improviser Simone Grande .
