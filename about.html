Andrea Trona is a sound designer, musician and programmer based in Bologna (IT), graduated in electroacoustic composition at the Cuneo Conservatory and graduating from the Bologna Conservatory in Sound Design. He works with sound in music and performance fields with a strong interest in performance and performance space, collaborating with artists and performers working as sound director and musician performing works in Italy and abroad. Since 2015 he collaborates with Lorenzo Donato producing See Closing Eyes and Liberty by Noah Pax, starting his first live electronic music experiences and participating in some electronic music festivals in Italy. He has been collaborating since 2019 with Elettronica Collettiva Bologna, the collective of the electronic music school of Bologna, and with the vocal quartet Rebel Bit, receiving awards and participating in European festivals. Since 2020 he has been collaborating permanently with musician and sound designer Francesco Vogli, with whom he developed the TRDmx device as part of a research grant promoted by the Centro Tempo Reale, created self-made light devices and recently founded immòto recordings, a project dedicated to audio recording. From 2021 he began collaborating on performance and installation projects with sound artist Agnese Banti, such as the performance I versi delle mani by choreographer Marta Bellu with dancer Laura Lucioli, the sound installation LUMINA and a new performance project to be presented in 2023. In 2022 he collaborates with Tempo Reale on the project XENAKIS | La Légende d'Eer and presents at Prima Onda Fest 22 the performance BOXE signed with the musician Federico Pipia. In 2023 he is scheduled to release his first electroacoustic improvisation album in duo with guitarist and improviser Simone Grande on the Never Anything Records label (US).
